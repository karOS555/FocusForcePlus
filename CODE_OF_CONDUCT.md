# Code of Conduct

## The short version

This project exists to help people who struggle with focus, so let's keep it a decent place to be.

Be respectful. Assume good faith. Disagree about ideas without attacking people. Remember there's a real person on the other end of every issue and pull request.

## Not okay

Harassment, insults, discrimination, or hostility of any kind. Personal attacks. Sharing someone's private information. Anything that makes the space unwelcoming.

## If something goes wrong

If someone crosses the line, report it privately through GitHub's Security tab (Report a vulnerability works for conduct reports too) or by contacting the maintainer through the repo. Reports are handled quietly and taken seriously. The maintainer can edit, remove, or block as needed.

## Scope

This applies everywhere the project lives: issues, pull requests, discussions, and anywhere people are representing the project.

This is loosely based on the spirit of the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), kept short on purpose.
